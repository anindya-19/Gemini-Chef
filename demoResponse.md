Since you have the staples of a great Italian-inspired base, I suggest making a **Classic Beef Bolognese Pasta**. This is a comforting, hearty dish that relies on your tomato paste and spices to build a deep, rich flavor profile.

***

# Classic Beef Bolognese

This recipe uses your ground beef and tomato paste, combined with your pantry spices, to create a restaurant-quality sauce.

*   **Prep time:** 5 minutes
*   **Cook time:** 20 minutes
*   **Servings:** 2–3

### Ingredients
*   **From your pantry:**
    *   Pasta (any shape works)
    *   Ground beef (approx. 1/2 lb to 1 lb)
    *   Tomato paste (2–3 tablespoons)
    *   Spices: Garlic powder, onion powder, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and black pepper.
    *   *Optional:* A pinch of red pepper flakes (if you like heat) and a pinch of sugar (to balance the acidity).
*   **Additional basics:**
    *   Cooking oil or butter
    *   Water (to help thin the sauce)

---

### Instructions

1.  **Boil the Pasta:** Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add your pasta and cook according to the package directions until *al dente*. Reserve about ½ cup of the pasta water before draining.
2.  **Brown the Beef:** While the water is heating, place a skillet over medium-high heat with a splash of oil. Add the ground beef. Season generously with salt, pepper, onion powder, and garlic powder. Break the beef apart with a spoon and cook until browned and no longer pink.
3.  **Create the Base:** Lower the heat to medium. Add the tomato paste directly to the beef. Stir it for 1–2 minutes; letting the paste "toast" slightly in the beef fat helps remove the tinny taste and makes the flavor more intense.
4.  **Simmer:** Stir in the dried oregano, basil, and a pinch of sugar (if using). Add about ¼ cup of the reserved pasta water to the beef and tomato mixture to create a silky sauce. Let it simmer gently for 3–5 minutes. If it looks too thick, add a little more pasta water.
5.  **Combine:** Toss the cooked pasta directly into the skillet with the sauce. Use tongs to coat the noodles thoroughly. 
6.  **Serve:** Taste and adjust your seasonings (more salt or spices as needed). Serve warm.

***

### Chef's Tip:
If you happen to have an **onion** or a **clove of garlic** in your kitchen, feel free to sauté them before adding the beef—it will add even more depth to the sauce!
